### **Part 3: Discussion**

**(Chủ đề: Technology & Society)**

**3. Question:** **How has technology changed the way people communicate in your country?**

**8.0+ Answer:**
"Technology has **fundamentally transformed communication** in my country, shifting it from predominantly face-to-face or letter-based interactions to an almost entirely digital landscape. Platforms like Zalo, Facebook, and various messaging apps have become ubiquitous, enabling instantaneous and constant connection regardless of geographical distance. This has fostered greater connectivity and the rapid dissemination of information, yet it also sometimes leads to a perceived reduction in the depth of personal interactions, with people perhaps spending less time engaging in meaningful conversations offline. It's a double-edged sword that has redefined social dynamics."

**4. Question:** **Do you think social media has a largely positive or negative impact on society?**

**8.0+ Answer:**
"That's a complex question, as social media presents both significant advantages and drawbacks. On the positive side, it serves as an incredibly powerful tool for **democratising information, fostering community building, and giving a voice to marginalised groups**, thereby driving social change. However, the negative impacts are equally profound, including the spread of misinformation, the erosion of privacy, and alarming rates of cyberbullying and mental health issues among users, particularly younger demographics. I believe its overall impact is still being debated, and largely depends on how individuals and regulators choose to harness or mitigate its immense power."

**5. Question:** **What role do you think governments should play in regulating technology, particularly social media?**

**8.0+ Answer:**
"Governments undoubtedly have a **crucial role to play in navigating the complexities of technological advancement**, especially concerning social media. Their responsibilities should encompass safeguarding user privacy, combating the proliferation of harmful content like hate speech and fake news, and ensuring fair competition within the digital marketplace. This could involve implementing stringent data protection laws, promoting digital literacy programmes, and potentially imposing penalties on platforms that fail to adhere to ethical guidelines. Striking the right balance between fostering innovation and protecting citizens' rights is paramount, requiring careful consideration to avoid stifling progress while still ensuring public welfare."
